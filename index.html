Multidisciplinary Design Project.It's all about teamwork.

Design a robot with sensory apparatus to explore a randomly constructed maze and to traverse between two designated points in the least possible amount of time

Rome wasn't built in a day, and neither was our robot. It took four months' team effort and countless ups and downs...I built a simulator using Java to experiment different algorithms as you can see in the figure, and finally implemented an intelligent exploration algorithm based on Depth-first Search for our robot. Why is it said intelligent? Two aspects:

  • The hardware given to us was in fact highly unreliable, which accounted for the occasional breakdowns during exploration. While a partial exploration might claim partial credits, a breakdown simply resulted in zero marks. As such, the robot was specified an exploration level: the percentage of the maze it should explore before it returned to the starting point prematurely.

  • When the robot got stuck in the maze, it needed to backtrack. It's easy to implement backtracking in the simulator by just poping from the stack; however, it may not be wise to follow the exact resersed path when it comes to the physical robot, because as the maze gets explored, a shorter backtracking path may exist. Therefore, we used A* search algorithm to find a shortest backtracking path.
